01

Automotive & EV Training Collaboration

IEG is strong in smart-factory and semiconductor training but has limited in-house automotive/EV hardware. Tesca's EV Technology Lab and automotive trainer lines appear to be a natural complement.

1.1 Beyond the BLDC-motor two-wheeler EV Training System, does Tesca offer EV kits for four-wheeler platforms, battery-pack/BMS training, or charging-infrastructure simulation?

1.2 Would Tesca be open to a "simulator-first" go-to-market model — where an IEG-developed EV curriculum/simulator is used with a prospective client to validate demand and budget before physical Tesca EV hardware is procured?

1.3 In which countries/institutions are Tesca's EV kits currently installed or under active proposal? (To check overlap with IEG's active markets: Central Asia, Southeast Asia, Middle East, Africa.)

1.4 Is white-label / co-branded packaging of EV kits possible for joint proposals?

1.5 What are typical MOQ, lead time, and indicative pricing bands for an EV lab setup (per-institution basis)?

02

Semiconductor Curriculum Extension

IEG's strength is back-end semiconductor process training (die bonding, wire bonding, molding, sawing, package testing) — a step ahead of Tesca's device-characteristics and SMD/PCB assembly trainers. We see this as sequential, not competitive.

2.1 Can you confirm whether Tesca currently offers any equipment covering wafer-level or package-level back-end semiconductor processes, beyond device-characteristics and SMD/PCB assembly trainers?

2.2 Would Tesca be interested in positioning a combined curriculum — semiconductor device fundamentals → IEG back-end packaging process training → Tesca SMD/PCB assembly — as a single proposal to shared institutional clients?

2.3 Are there existing Tesca clients (universities, polytechnics, TVET centers) currently using your electronics/embedded trainers who may be candidates for this kind of curriculum upgrade?

03

Channel & Commercial Structure

To scope a workable brokerage / referral arrangement for both the EV and semiconductor tracks.

3.1 Would Tesca be open to a referral or brokerage arrangement in which IEG introduces qualified opportunities and Tesca (for EV/automotive) or IEG (for semiconductor back-end) fulfills the equipment supply, with a commission on closed deals?

3.2 What commercial structure has worked well for Tesca in past channel-partner arrangements — a referral fee, a distributor margin built into pricing, or a formal consortium/JV split?

3.3 Are there territories where Tesca would prefer exclusivity, and territories where open/non-exclusive collaboration would be acceptable?

04

Organizational Capacity & ODA Track Record

To help us jointly scope realistic project sizes for multilateral or government-funded bids.

4.1 Could you share 1–2 recent examples of Tesca-delivered TVET/education projects funded by the World Bank, ADB, AfDB, or GOI Line of Credit — including approximate project value and country?

4.2 For a multi-country or multi-year consortium bid (e.g., KOICA IBS, EDCF, or similar tri-lateral development cooperation), what is Tesca's typical internal project-execution team size and structure?

4.3 Would Tesca be interested in exploring a joint bid on an upcoming KOICA- or EDCF-funded TVET modernization project, with IEG as technical/equipment lead and Tesca contributing regional channel and turnkey project-delivery experience?

05

Geographic Footprint — North America, Europe & Brazil

Public records show only isolated, single-item shipments to the US, France, and Iceland (with no named institutional partner), a larger 171-unit Power Electronics Trainer project in Peru, and an ESC India trade-delegation visit to São Paulo (Brazil) with no confirmed sale on record. We'd like to understand the real picture before assuming these are established channels.

5.1 In North America and Europe, are there any active institutional relationships (universities, TVET centers, or distributors) beyond one-off equipment shipments? If so, which countries/institutions would you highlight?

5.2 Does Tesca have a local distributor, agent, or representative office in the US or in any EU/EEA country? If not, how are inquiries and after-sales support currently handled in these regions?

5.3 Following the São Paulo Buyer-Seller Meet, has Tesca converted any Brazilian opportunities into an actual order or installation? What is the current status of Brazil market entry, and would there be interest in exploring it jointly?

5.4 The Peru project (171 Power Electronics Trainers) appears to be your largest documented single deployment outside Asia/Africa/Middle East. Could you share: (a) roughly when this was delivered, (b) how the opportunity was sourced (direct tender, distributor, ODA-funded), and (c) whether Tesca still has an active relationship with this institution today?

5.5 If the relationship with the Peru institution is still active, would Tesca be open to introducing IEG as a follow-on partner for a curriculum upgrade path — moving the institution from foundational power electronics training toward smart-factory and PLC-based automation training?
